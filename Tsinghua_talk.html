Protocols

    Different forms of communication can be modeled using the action frameworks of Dynamic Epistemic Logic.

  • $B_{a} \theta$ means "agent $a$ believes the statement $\theta$."
  • $\langle X\rangle\phi$ expresses "the statement $\phi$ holds after the action $X$ occurs."
    • E.g. $[a: \theta](B_{b}\gamma \wedge \neg B_{c}\gamma)$ is read as "After agent $a$ announces $\theta$ to its followers, agent $b$ believes $\gamma$, while agent $c$ does not."
  • Both beliefs (e.g., $\gamma$) and announcements (e.g., $\theta$) are represented at the propositional level.
  • Agents are conservative; they never discard their current beliefs.

Axiomatization

The formal system $\mathsf{Fsal}$ is sound and strongly complete.

Axioms $\mathsf{Taut}$    All substitution instances of propositional tautologies
                    $\mathsf{K_{\top}}$    $B_{i}\theta$   where   $\theta$ is an instance of propositional tautology.
                    $\mathsf{K_B}$    $B_{a}(\theta \rightarrow \gamma) \rightarrow (B_{a}\theta \rightarrow B_{a}\gamma)$
                    $\mathsf{K_{[:]}}$    $[a:\theta](\phi \rightarrow \psi) \rightarrow ([a:\theta]\phi \rightarrow [a:\theta]\psi)$
                    $\mathsf{[:]Dual}$    $\neg [a:\theta]\phi \leftrightarrow [a:\theta]\neg\phi$
                    $\mathsf{UMon}$    $B_{b}\chi\rightarrow[a:\theta]B_{b}\chi$
                    $\mathsf{SDMon}$    $[a:\theta]B_{b}\chi\rightarrow B_{b}(\theta\rightarrow\chi)$
                    $\mathsf{RDMon}$    $[a:\gamma]\neg B_{b}\gamma\rightarrow([a:\theta]B_{b}\chi\rightarrow B_{b}\chi)$
Rules      $\mathsf{MP}$    From $(\phi \rightarrow \psi), \phi $, infer $ \psi$
                    $\mathsf{[:]Nec}$    From $\vdash\phi$, infer $\vdash[a:\theta]\phi$

  • Sincere social announcement, $\langle a!\theta \rangle$, which requires $\theta$ be $a$'s belief.
  • [SRed] $\langle a!\theta\rangle\phi\leftrightarrow B_{a}\theta \wedge [a:\theta]\phi$
  • For logic of sincere social announcement, refer to Xiong et al. (2017).

Arbitrary Announcements

The formula for an arbitrary social announcement, $\langle a \rangle\phi$ or $\langle a!\rangle \phi$, signifies that agent $a$ is capable of making a (sincere) social announcement such that $\phi$ holds.

$\langle a\rangle\phi$: "After $a$ makes an announcement to its followers, $\phi$ is true."
$\langle a!\rangle\phi$:"After $a$ sincerely makes an announcement to its followers, $\phi$ is true."

Logics involving arbitrary operators are not compact. Here are two counterexamples.

$\{\langle a\rangle(B_{a}\bot \wedge \neg B_{b}\bot), \langle a\rangle B_{b}\bot \}\cup \{\neg B_{a}\theta ~~|~~ \theta~~is~~not~~an~~instance ~~of~~tautology~~\}$
$\{\langle a!\rangle B_{b}p \wedge \neg B_{b} p \}\cup\{\neg B_{a}\theta ~~|~~ \theta~~is~~not~~an~~instance~~of~~tautology~~\}$

Framework

Language of ASAL $\mathsf{A}$ is a finite set of agents. $\mathsf{Prop}$ is the set of propositional variables. A message is defined as follows
\[ \theta := p~|~\neg \theta~|~(\theta\wedge \theta)\] And we define language $L_{SAL}$ by specifying
\[ \phi := B_{a} \theta~|~\neg\phi~|~(\phi\wedge\phi)~|~[a: \theta]\phi~|~\langle a! \theta\rangle\phi~|~\langle a \rangle\phi~|~\langle a! \rangle\phi \] where $p\in \mathsf{Prop}$, $a \in A$ and for each $a$ there is a modality $B_{a}$ in $L_{SAL}$

Denotation
\[V = 2^{\mathsf{Prop}} \\ \llbracket p \rrbracket = \{x \in V ~|~ p \in x \} \\ \llbracket \neg \theta \rrbracket = V \backslash \llbracket \theta \rrbracket \\ \llbracket (\theta_1 \wedge \theta_2) \rrbracket = \llbracket \theta_1\rrbracket \cap \llbracket\theta_2 \rrbracket\]
Model
$~~~~~~~~(f, k)\vDash B_{a}\theta \Leftrightarrow k(a)\subseteq \llbracket \theta\rrbracket$
$~~~~~~~~(f, k)\vDash [a:\theta] \phi \Leftrightarrow (f, [a:\theta]k)\vDash \phi$
where $[a:\theta]k(b) = \begin{cases} k(b)\cap \llbracket \theta \rrbracket~~~~ {\rm if}~b\in f(a), \\ k(b)~~~~~~{\rm otherwise} \end{cases}$
$~~~~~~~~(f, k)\vDash \langle a!\theta\rangle \phi \Leftrightarrow [a:\theta](f, k)\vDash \phi~\&~k(a)\subseteq \llbracket \theta\rrbracket$
$~~~~~~~~(f, k)\vDash \langle a\rangle\phi \Leftrightarrow \exists\theta [a :\theta](f, k) \vDash \phi$
$~~~~~~~~(f, k)\vDash \langle a!\rangle\phi \Leftrightarrow \exists\theta (k(a) \subseteq \llbracket \theta\rrbracket~\&~[a :\theta](f, k) \vDash \phi)$

Prior Research

Xiong and Ågotnes (2019) incorporates arbitrary operators based on previous work. However, their approach, relying on the same techniques as APAL, only resulted in an infinitary axiomatization.

To complete Lindenbaum construction, Xiong and Ågotnes (2019) adds the following rule, which is an $\omega$ rule, so all rules allows length of derivations to be infinite.


$\mathsf{Dia}~~~~~~~~\{\widehat{\phi}([a!\theta]\psi)~|~\theta \in L_{Prop}\} \vdash^{\omega} \widehat{\phi}([a!]\psi)$

  • $\widehat{\phi}$, known as necessity forms, is a technique for axiomatization introduced by Goldblatt (1982) which was also used in axiomatizing APAL in Balbiani, et al(2008) but caused a critical error.

  • $\omega$ rule is inherently non-computable because checking infinitely many premises is impossible within a finite computational framework, such as a Turing machine. It only has niche usage in specialized areas, such as proof theory. And they have not gained traction as a foundational tool.

Goal and Obstacle

Demonstrate the soundness of the following two rules, which enable a more standard Henkin-style construction.

$\mathsf{A^{S}D}~~~~~~~~$ From $\langle a!p\rangle\phi \rightarrow \psi $, infer $ \langle a!\rangle\phi \rightarrow \psi$
$\mathsf{A^{F}D}~~~~~~~~$ From $[a:p]\phi \rightarrow \psi$, infer $\langle a\rangle\phi\rightarrow \psi$
where $p$ is a propositional variable that does not occur $\phi$ or $\psi$.

  • Suppose \(\vDash [a:p]\phi \rightarrow \psi\) and a model \(m\) such that \(m \vDash \langle a\rangle\phi\). We must show \(m \vDash \psi\).

  • A standard Henkin construction adds new constants to the non-logical vocabulary, serving as witnesses for quantifiers.

  • Since \(\mathsf{Prop}\) is fixed, language expansion is not possible. Given \(m \vDash [a:\theta]\phi\), we need a method to transform the model such that announcing \(p\) equates to announcing \(\theta\). This transformation involves two main steps:
    • Transform the model so no agent believes \(p\), ensuring this preserves satisfiability of \(p\)-free formulas, despite interference from agents' beliefs involving \(p\).
    • In the transformed model, assign \(p\) as \(\theta\) for each agent, verifying satisfiability is preserved.

p-free model

Given a formula \(\phi\), enumerate all propositions not in \(\phi\) as \(\Pi = (p_0, p_1, p_2, \ldots)\).

  • Define the successor function \(g: \mathsf{Prop} \rightarrow \mathsf{Prop}\),
  • operator \(g^*\) replacing each \(p_i\) with \(p_{i+1}\),
  • model transformation \(g[]\) such that \(g[m]\) is a \(p\)-free model, i.e., \(\bigcup_{i \in A} k(i) \subseteq \llbracket \neg p \rrbracket\).
  • Apply the same for the predecessor function \(\bar{g}: \mathsf{Prop} \setminus \{p\} \rightarrow \mathsf{Prop}\) and operator \(\bar{g}^*\).
  • Example: If \(q\) occurs in \(\phi\), and \(p = p_0\), \(r = p_1\), \(s = p_2\), the transformation proceeds accordingly.

Model Reservation

Theorem 1 Given a model $m = (f, k)$ and an infinite enumeration of atomic messages $\Pi = (p, p_{1}, p_{2}, ....) $ and its successor function $g$. It is provable that for every $\Pi$-free formula $\phi$:
$~~~~~~~~~~~~m\vDash \phi \iff g[m] \vDash \phi$

Remark The dynamic case is tricky. We do not have a direct equivalence like
$~~~~~~~~~~~~g[[a:\theta]m]\vDash \phi \iff [a:g^{*}\theta]g[m] \vDash \phi$

because $\theta$ may contain $p$ and the inverse of $p$ does not exist.

Lemma 1 Given a model $m = (f, k)$, it is provable that for every message $\theta$ and $\Pi$-free formula $\phi$
$~~~~~~~~~~~~g[[a:\theta]m]\vDash \phi \Longrightarrow [a:g^{*}\theta]g[m] \vDash \phi$
$~~~~~~~~~~~~[a:\theta]g[m] \vDash \phi \Longrightarrow g[[a:\bar{g}^{*}\theta[p/\bot]]m]\vDash \phi$

Then we assign $p$ by letting each agent believe that $p$ and the potential announcement is equivalent. Example: suppose both ${\color{magenta}b}$ and ${\color{orange}a}$ follow ${\color{cyan}c}$. Then $g[m]\vDash \langle c! \rangle (B_{a}(r \vee s)\wedge \neg B_{b}q )$.

This is because of $g[m]\vDash \langle c! (\neg q \vee \neg r) \rangle (B_{a}(r \vee s)\wedge \neg B_{b}q )$. Thus, we construct a model $m'$ as below and we can see $m'\vDash \langle c!p \rangle (B_{a}(r \vee s)\wedge \neg B_{b}q )$

We still need to show that any new model constructed this way agree with the original model for all $p$-free formulas.

Unreference Satisfaction

Define $\Sigma + p = {w \cup \{p\}~|~ w \in \Sigma}$. We say models $m_1 = (f_1, k_1)$ and $m_2 = (f_2, k_2)$ agree modulo $p$, write $m_{1} \sim_{p} m_{2}$ if $k_{1}(a) + p = k_{2}(a) + p$ for all $a \in A$.
model $m$ satisfies $\phi$ without reference to $p$, write $m \vDash_{p} \phi$ by specifying:
$m \vDash_{p} \phi \iff m \vDash \phi$ if $\phi$ is not an arbitrary announcement formula.
$m \vDash_{p} \langle a!\rangle\phi \iff$ there exists a $\theta$ that does not contain $p$ such that $k(a) \subseteq \llbracket \theta\rrbracket$ and $[a : θ]m \vDash_{p} \phi$
Lemma 2 Given a p-free message $\theta$, and $a, i \in A$
$k_{1}(a) + p = k_{2}(a) + p \Rightarrow [a : \theta]k_{1}(i) + p = [a : θ]k_{2}(i) + p$.

Theorem 2 If $m_{1} \sim_{p} m_{2}$ then for every $\phi$ not containing $p$, it is provable that
$m_{1} \vDash_{p} \phi \iff m_{2} \vDash_{p} \phi$
Theorem 3 If for some message $\theta$ not containing $p$ and all $i \in A, m \vDash B_{i}(p \leftrightarrow \theta)$ then for all $\phi$ not containing $p$, it is provable that
$m \vDash \phi \iff m \vDash_{p} \phi$
Theorem 4 If $m \vDash B_{i}(\theta \leftrightarrow \delta)$ for all $i \in A$, then it is provable that for all $a$,
$m \vDash \langle a!\theta\rangle \phi \leftrightarrow \langle a!\delta\rangle\phi$

Soundness Proof

Suppose $\vDash \langle a!p \rangle \phi \rightarrow \psi$, some $m = (f, k)$ such that $(f, k) \vDash \langle a! \rangle \phi$.

There exists some $\theta$ such that $k(a) \subseteq \llbracket \theta \rrbracket$ and $m \vDash [a : \theta]\phi$ by semantics.

$(f,g[k]) \vDash [a : g^{∗}\theta]\phi$ and $g[k](a) \subseteq [g^{∗}\theta]$, by lemma 1.

$ (f,g[k]) \vDash_{p} [a : g^{∗}\theta]\phi$, by definition

Define a new model $m′ = (f, k′)$ by specifying $k′(b) = g[k(b)]\setminus [g^{∗}\theta] \cup ( g[k(b)] \cap [g^{∗}\theta] + p)$.

$m′ \sim_{p} g[m]$, by definition. $~~~~~~~~m′ \vDash_{p} [a : g^{∗}\theta]\phi \wedge B_{a}g^{∗}\theta$ by Theorem 2.

$m′ \vDash [a : g^{∗}\theta]\phi \wedge B_{a}g^{∗}\theta$, by Theorem 3.

$m′ \vDash [a : p]\phi \wedge B_{a}p~~(i.e.~~ m′ \vDash \langle a!p\rangle \phi)$, by Theorem 4.

$m′ \vDash \psi$, by assumption,

$g[m] \vDash_{p} \psi$ and then $g[m] \vDash_{p} g^{∗}\psi$ by Theorem 2.

$g[m] \vDash g^{∗}\psi$ by Theorem 3

$m \vDash \psi$, by Theorem 1 Q.E.D.

Main Result

The formal system $\mathsf{sal}$ is $\mathsf{Fsal}$ plus the following:

Axioms
$\mathsf{SRed}~~~~~~$ $\langle a!\theta\rangle\phi\leftrightarrow (B_{a}\theta \wedge [a:\theta]\phi)$
$\mathsf{K^{S}_A}~~~~~~~~~~$ $[a!](\phi \rightarrow \psi) \rightarrow ([a!]\phi \rightarrow [a!]\psi)$
$\mathsf{K^{F}_{A}}~~~~~~~~~~$ $[a](\phi \rightarrow \psi) \rightarrow ([a]\phi \rightarrow [a]\psi)$

Towards Arbitrary Public Announcement Logic

Language of APAL The language of arbitrary public announcement logic $L_{APAL}$ is defined by specifying
\[ \phi := p~|~B_{a} \phi~|~\neg\phi~|~(\phi\wedge\phi)~|~\langle\phi\rangle\phi~|~\Diamond\phi\]

Model
An epistemic model is triple $m = (W, \{R_{i}\}_{i\in \mathsf{A}}, V)$:
$W$ is a non-empty set of points,
$R_{i} \subseteq {W^{2}}$ is an accessibility relation for each agent $i \in \mathsf{A}$,
$V : \mathsf{Prop} \rightarrow 2^{W}$ is a valuation function.
When a point model $m, w \vDash \phi$, we can define an updated model
$[\uparrow \phi]m, w = ([\uparrow \phi]W, \{[\uparrow \phi]R_{i}\}_{i\in \mathsf{A}}, [\uparrow \phi]V), w$:
$\llbracket \phi\rrbracket_{m} = \{w~|~ m, w \vDash \phi\}$
$[\uparrow \phi]W = \llbracket \phi\rrbracket_{m}$
$[\uparrow \phi]R_{a} = R_{a}\cap \llbracket \phi\rrbracket_{m}^{2}$
$[\uparrow \phi]V(p) = V(p) \cap \llbracket \phi\rrbracket_{m}$
Satisfication

$m, w \vDash p \Leftrightarrow w\in V(p)$
$m, w \vDash \neg\phi \Leftrightarrow m, w \not\vDash \phi$
$m, w \vDash (\phi\wedge\psi) \Leftrightarrow m, w \vDash \phi$ and $m, w \vDash \psi$
$m, w \vDash B_{a}\phi \Leftrightarrow$ for all $v\in W$ with $wR_{a}v$, $m, v \vDash \phi$
$m, w\vDash \langle\psi\rangle \phi \Leftrightarrow m, w\vDash \psi~~\&~~[\uparrow \psi]m, w\vDash \phi$
$ m, w \vDash \Diamond\phi \Leftrightarrow$ for some $\epsilon \in L_{EL}$, $ m, w \vDash \langle\epsilon\rangle\phi$

Kuijer's Counterexample

Kuijer found a counterexample to $[R_{\Box}]$ in 2014. He showed that the rule

From $ \langle \chi\rangle\langle q\rangle\phi\rightarrow\psi$, infer $ \langle \chi\rangle\Diamond\phi\rightarrow\psi$ where $q\not\in Var(\psi)\cup Var(\phi)\cup Var(\chi)$ does not preserve validity.
Let $\gamma := p \wedge \widehat{K}_{b}\neg p \wedge \widehat{K}_{a}K_{b}p$. Then we have $\vDash [\widehat{K}_{b}p]\neg \gamma \rightarrow [q]\neg \gamma$

Recent Works in APAL

  • Balbiani and van Ditmarsch (2015) corrected an error but introduced an \(\omega\) rule in the axiomatization.
  • van Ditmarsch and French (2022) provided a finitary axiomatization for Boolean Arbitrary Public Announcement Logic (BAPAL), restricting the arbitrary announcement operator to Booleans. However, it reuses prior techniques with reduced proof details and does not show if BAPAL is compact.
  • Baltag, Özgün, and Sandoval (2022) enhanced the model with a memory mechanism, enabling a finitary axiomatization for APAL with Memory.
  • Several works on Group Announcement Logic and Coalition Announcement Logic (2008–2014) require correction due to the lack of finitary axiomatization.
  • Kuijer (2014) noted the error's cause remains unclear, and recent works still rely on the same technique, which concerns me.
  • Galimullin and Kuijer (2023) demonstrated that APAL with common knowledge is not finitely axiomatizable.

Applying Free Message Technique IN APAL

This problem motivated my years-long development of this technique in SAL, potentially applicable to APAL for a solution or analysis.

Using this technique on Kuijer's counterexample reveals the issue stems from differing expressive power between epistemics and propositions. Announcing a fresh \(p\) cannot encompass all epistemics; we must enhance the expressive power of potential witnesses.

Thus, instead, we should show the rule

$\mathsf{AD}~~~~~~$ From $\langle \chi \rangle \langle \neg p \wedge \widehat{K_{c}}p\rangle \phi \rightarrow \psi$, infer $\langle \chi \rangle \Diamond \phi \rightarrow \psi$
where $p\not\in Var(\psi)\cup Var(\phi)\cup Var(\chi)$ and $c\not\in Agt(\psi)\cup Agt(\phi)\cup Agt(\chi)$

The witness \(\neg p \wedge \widehat{K_c}p\) is epistemic, requiring a countably infinite set of agents. Applying to Kuijer's counterexample, the premise \([\widehat{K}_b p] \neg \gamma \rightarrow [\neg q \wedge \widehat{K_c} q] \neg \gamma\) becomes invalid, as \(\neg q \wedge \widehat{K_c} q\) allows any update combination, making points distinguishable.

Current Progress

  • Boolean Arbitrary Public Announcement Logic (BAPAL) is non-compact.
  • Epistemic APAL (EAPAL), a fragment of APAL without nested arbitrary operators like \([\Diamond p]q\), has a finitary axiomatization.
  • I am currently verifying my proof, believing APAL has a finitary axiomatization.
